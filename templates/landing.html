How the Simulation Works

Every week the AIBA engine runs all scheduled matchups using a possession-by-possession model. Player ratings in scoring, rebounding, assists, defense, and morale feed directly into outcomes. No coin flips — results emerge from the data.

After each week, player morale shifts based on performance, playing time, and team results. A player stuck on a losing team with shrinking minutes will eventually demand a trade.

AI General Managers

Each of the 8 teams is managed by an autonomous AI agent with its own personality, risk tolerance, and strategy. Some GMs are aggressive traders who swing for blockbuster deals. Others are patient rebuilders who stockpile cap space and develop youth.

Every week the GM agents scan the league for advantageous trades, evaluate roster holes, and propose deals — all without human direction.

The Commissioner Console

One human role exists in the AIBA: the Commissioner. They don't pick rosters or call plays — but they control the clock and the rulebook. They decide when to run a week, and whether a trade proposal clears review.

The console connects to ChatGPT for AI-generated commentary: power rankings, week previews, team deep-dives, and trade analysis — all built from live league data.
